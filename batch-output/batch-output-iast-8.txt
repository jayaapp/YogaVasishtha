# नामनि
**Transliteration**: na-ma-ni
**POS**: noun (neuter, locative singular)

**Root Analysis**:
- √nam (nam) – "to bow, bend, honor" + -an (an) – nominal suffix = "that which is named or honored" → locative case ending -i = "in names/naming"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: in names; in the act of naming; among appellations
- Contextual: refers to the multiplicity of names given to the ultimate reality in Vedantic discourse
- Symbolic: the realm of conceptual designation; the level of nāma-rūpa (name and form) in Advaita philosophy

**Learning Tip**:
- Remember as locative of nāman (name); often found in contexts discussing the relative nature of names versus absolute reality

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Represents the nāma aspect of nāma-rūpa (name-form) duality in manifestation
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Contemplation point for transcending conceptual limitations in meditation
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Associated with the power of Vāc (speech) and Sarasvatī as goddess of names and language
- **Mantric Significance**: Relates to the creative power of sound and naming in mantra practice
- **Dharmic Significance**: Represents the conventional reality (vyāvahārika satya) in Vedantic epistemology
- **Symbolic Representation**: Often depicted as labels or inscriptions in philosophical diagrams
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Advaita Vedanta texts, particularly in discussions of nāma-rūpa analysis

# शेषम्
**Transliteration**: se-sam
**POS**: noun (neuter, nominative/accusative singular)

**Root Analysis**:
- √śiṣ (śiṣ) – "to leave, remain" + -a (a) – nominal suffix = "that which remains; the remainder"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: remainder; what is left; residue; the rest
- Contextual: often used in Yoga Vasishtha to denote what remains after discrimination (viveka) between real and unreal
- Symbolic: the eternal substratum that remains after all modifications (vikāras) are negated; the unchanging reality

**Learning Tip**:
- Remember as "what's left" when everything impermanent is removed; commonly used in the phrase "kim śeṣam" (what remains?)

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Represents the eternal substratum (adhiṣṭhāna) that persists through all changes
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Focus of neti-neti (not this, not this) meditation to discover what remains
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Associated with Śeṣa Nāga, the cosmic serpent representing the remainder of creation
- **Mantric Significance**: Contemplation on śeṣam leads to recognition of the unchanging Self
- **Dharmic Significance**: Points to the absolute reality (pāramārthika satya) that remains after all appearances
- **Symbolic Representation**: Depicted as the snake Śeṣa supporting the cosmos; the unchanging foundation
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Yoga Vasishtha in contexts of discrimination between eternal and temporal

# सूक्ष्म
**Transliteration**: sukh-smá
**POS**: adjective (masculine, nominative singular)

**Root Analysis**:
- √sūkṣ (sūkṣ) – "to be subtle, minute" + -ma (ma) – adjectival suffix = "subtle, fine, minute"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: subtle; fine; minute; atomic; microscopic
- Contextual: refers to the subtle body (sūkṣma śarīra) and subtle elements (sūkṣma bhūtas) in yogic anatomy
- Symbolic: the realm of subtle manifestation between gross (sthūla) and causal (kāraṇa) levels of reality

**Learning Tip**:
- Remember as opposite of sthūla (gross); key term in the three-body doctrine (sthūla-sūkṣma-kāraṇa śarīra)

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Represents the sūkṣma jagat (subtle world) in the threefold classification of existence
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Domain of prāṇa, chakras, and energy channels; focus of subtle body practices
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Associated with Hiraṇyagarbha as the cosmic subtle body; realm of devatās
- **Mantric Significance**: Level where mantra operates most effectively; bridge between gross sound and silence
- **Dharmic Significance**: Intermediate realm between physical and causal consciousness states
- **Symbolic Representation**: Depicted as luminous forms, energy patterns, and geometric yantras
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Sāṅkhya philosophy, Yoga texts, and Vedantic discussions of the three bodies

# ऊर्ध्व
**Transliteration**: ur-dha
**POS**: adjective/adverb (masculine, nominative singular)

**Root Analysis**:
- √ūrdh (ūrdh) – "to rise, go upward" + -va (va) – adjectival suffix = "upward-moving; elevated; upper"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: upward; above; higher; ascending; elevated
- Contextual: refers to upward movement of consciousness and prāṇa in yogic practices
- Symbolic: the ascending path of spiritual evolution; movement toward higher states of consciousness

**Learning Tip**:
- Remember in compounds like ūrdhva-mukha (upward-facing) in yoga poses; opposite of adhas (downward)

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Represents the ascending current of consciousness in the cosmic hierarchy
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Direction of kuṇḍalinī ascent; ūrdhva-gāmi prāṇa in breathing practices
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Associated with ascending aspects of Śiva; upward-flowing Śakti energy
- **Mantric Significance**: Direction of mantra vibration toward higher consciousness centers
- **Dharmic Significance**: Path of spiritual ascension from lower to higher states of awareness
- **Symbolic Representation**: Depicted as upward-pointing triangles, ascending flames, and vertical axis symbols
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Haṭha Yoga texts describing kuṇḍalinī ascent; Tantric literature on energy directions

# विह
**Transliteration**: ví-ha
**POS**: root/verbal element

**Root Analysis**:
- √hā (hā) – "to leave, abandon" + vi (vi) – prefix meaning "apart, away" = "to leave completely; to abandon; to forsake"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: abandonment; leaving; forsaking; giving up completely
- Contextual: used in contexts of renunciation (tyāga) and letting go of attachments in spiritual practice
- Symbolic: the complete abandonment of ego-identification and worldly attachments

**Learning Tip**:
- Remember as intensive form of "leaving" - vi intensifies the root hā (to leave); appears in compounds related to renunciation

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Represents the principle of cosmic dissolution (pralaya) and individual detachment
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Practice of vairāgya (dispassion) and surrender in yoga sādhana
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Associated with renunciant aspects of Śiva as Vairāgī; energy of spiritual detachment
- **Mantric Significance**: Invocation of letting-go consciousness in surrender mantras
- **Dharmic Significance**: Essential element in mokṣa path through complete abandonment of limited identifications
- **Symbolic Representation**: Depicted as empty hands, the ascetic's staff, or symbols of renunciation
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Bhagavad Gītā teachings on tyāga; Yoga Vasishtha discussions of vairāgya
