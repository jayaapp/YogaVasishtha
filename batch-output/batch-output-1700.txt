# समाणाः
**Transliteration**: sa-mā-ṇāḥ (sa-mā-ṇāḥ)
**POS**: Adjective, masculine plural nominative

**Root Analysis**:
- From सम् (sam) meaning "together, equal" + आणा (āṇā) suffix indicating similarity or equality
- Forms comparative or descriptive plural adjective

**Meanings**:
- Literal: Similar ones, equal ones, those of the same kind
- Contextual: Beings or entities of equal status or nature in spiritual discourse
- Symbolic: Unity in diversity, sameness of essential nature

**Learning Tip**:
- Remember "sama" = same/equal; the plural form indicates multiple entities sharing equality

# समाधि
**Transliteration**: sa-mā-dhi (sa-mā-dhi)
**POS**: Noun, masculine

**Root Analysis**:
- From सम् (sam) "together" + आ (ā) "toward" + धा (dhā) "to place/hold"
- Literally means "placing together" or "complete absorption"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: Complete absorption, concentration, trance
- Contextual: The highest state of meditative consciousness in Yoga Vasishtha
- Symbolic: Union of individual consciousness with universal consciousness

**Learning Tip**:
- Think "sama" (same) + "dhi" (intellect) = mind becoming one with object of meditation

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: The culminating state of yogic practice where duality dissolves
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: The eighth limb of Patanjali's Ashtanga Yoga
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Associated with Shiva as the supreme meditator
- **Mantric Significance**: The state where mantra, meaning, and meditator unite
- **Dharmic Significance**: The direct realization of one's true nature as pure consciousness
- **Symbolic Representation**: Often depicted as the thousand-petaled lotus at the crown
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Yoga Sutras, Yoga Vasishtha, Bhagavad Gita

**POS**: Complex compound, neuter

**Root Analysis**:
- सूर्य (sūrya) + दास (dāsa) + समत (samata)
- Possibly "equality with solar servants" or complex solar reference

**Meanings**:
- Literal: Solar servant equality, comparison with sun's attendants
- Contextual: Some form of equality or comparison involving solar principles
- Symbolic: Hierarchical relationship within solar cosmic principles

**Learning Tip**:
- Complex compound involving solar references and equality/service concepts

# सृष्टि
**Transliteration**: sṛṣ-ṭi (sṛṣ-ṭi)
**POS**: Noun, feminine

**Root Analysis**:
- From सृज् (sṛj) "to create, emit" + ति (ti) suffix
- The act or result of creation

**Meanings**:
- Literal: Creation, manifestation, universe
- Contextual: The cosmic process of bringing forth the manifest world
- Symbolic: The eternal creative activity of consciousness

**Learning Tip**:
- "Sṛṣṭi" = creation; from root "sṛj" meaning to create/emit

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: The creative phase of cosmic cycles (sṛṣṭi-sthiti-laya)
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Understanding creation helps in meditation on cosmic principles
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Associated with Brahmā as creator and Śakti as creative power
- **Dharmic Significance**: The emergence of multiplicity from the one absolute
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Purāṇas, Vedānta texts, creation cosmologies
