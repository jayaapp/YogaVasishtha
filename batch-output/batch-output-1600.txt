# शब्द
**Transliteration**: śab-da
**POS**: Masculine noun

**Root Analysis**:
- From root √śabh (sound, noise) + suffix -da
- Related to śabdāya (to make sound), śabdita (sounded)

**Meanings**:
- Literal: Sound, word, noise
- Contextual: Sacred sound, divine word, cosmic vibration
- Symbolic: The primordial sound from which creation emerges

**Learning Tip**:
- Remember "śabda" as the fundamental sound principle in creation

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: The vibrational foundation of manifest reality
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Mantra practice and sound meditation
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Associated with Sarasvatī and Vāc (divine speech)
- **Mantric Significance**: The principle underlying all sacred sounds
- **Dharmic Significance**: Sound as consciousness expressing itself
- **Symbolic Representation**: Often shown as sound waves or AUM symbol
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Vedas, Yoga Vasishtha, Spanda Kārikā
