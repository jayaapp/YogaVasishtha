# १
**Transliteration**: eka
**POS**: number (cardinal)

**Root Analysis**:
- Not derived from a verbal root. Functions as a numeral.

**Meanings**:
- Literal: one, single, alone
- Contextual: fundamental unity, the One principle in Vedanta
- Symbolic: non-duality (advaita), ultimate reality

**Learning Tip**:
- Foundation of all counting; remember as the source from which all multiplicity appears

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Represents the absolute Brahman, the undifferentiated consciousness
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Unity consciousness in samādhi states
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Īśvara as the One without a second
- **Mantric Significance**: Foundation of numerical mantras and sacred geometry
- **Dharmic Significance**: The goal of all spiritual practice - realizing oneness
- **Symbolic Representation**: Single point (bindu) in yantras representing consciousness
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Upaniṣads (ekam evādvitīyam)

# ४र्थ
**Transliteration**: ar-tha
**POS**: noun (masculine)

**Root Analysis**:
- √ṛ (ṛ) – "to go, move, reach" + suffix -tha = "that which is reached or obtained"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: meaning, purpose, wealth, object
- Contextual: one of the four goals of life (puruṣārtha)
- Symbolic: material prosperity balanced with dharma

**Learning Tip**:
- Remember as "that which is reached" - goals and purposes

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: One of four puruṣārthas, material aspect of existence
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Maṇipūra cakra associations with prosperity
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Lakṣmī as goddess of wealth and prosperity
- **Mantric Significance**: Śrī mantras for abundance
- **Dharmic Significance**: Legitimate pursuit when aligned with dharma
- **Symbolic Representation**: Lotus, cow, gold, jewels
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Arthaśāstra, Dharmaśāstra texts
