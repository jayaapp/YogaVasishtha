# उशन्
**Transliteration**: u-śan
**POS**: noun (masculine)

**Root Analysis**:
- From √उष् (uṣ) "to burn" = "burning one, Uśanas" (name of sage)

**Meanings**:
- Literal: The burning one, Uśanas (sage and preceptor of demons)
- Contextual: Ancient sage mentioned in Yoga Vasishtha lineages
- Symbolic: Wisdom that burns away ignorance

**Learning Tip**:
- Uśanas/Śukrācārya was guru of the asuras and master of Mṛtasañjīvanī vidyā

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Wisdom principle that transforms through spiritual fire
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Knowledge that burns through spiritual obstacles
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Śukra as teacher of transformative wisdom
- **Mantric Significance**: Mantras invoking transformative spiritual knowledge
- **Dharmic Significance**: Wisdom that transcends conventional moral categories
- **Symbolic Representation**: Spiritual fire, transformative flame
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Purāṇas, astrological texts, Yoga Vasishtha
