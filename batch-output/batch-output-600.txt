# जन्माद्यस्ययतः
**Transliteration**: jan-mā-dyas-ya-ya-taḥ
**POS**: compound phrase from Brahma Sūtras

**Root Analysis**:
- जन्म (janma) – "birth" + आदि (ādi) – "etc." + अस्य (asya) – "of this" + यत् (yat) – "from which" + तः (taḥ) – "from that" = "from which (comes) birth etc. of this (universe)"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: from which comes birth etc. of this (universe)
- Contextual: The opening sūtra defining Brahman as the source of cosmic manifestation
- Symbolic: Points to the ultimate source from which all existence emerges

**Learning Tip**:
- This is Brahma Sūtra 1.1.2, the first definition of Brahman

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Brahman as the material and efficient cause of the universe
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Understanding the source leads to merger with that source
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Brahman as both transcendent and immanent reality
- **Mantric Significance**: The ultimate referent of all sacred sounds and mantras
- **Dharmic Significance**: Establishing Brahman as the foundation of all existence and ethics
- **Symbolic Representation**: The seed from which the cosmic tree grows
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Brahma Sūtra 1.1.2, fundamental definition of Brahman

- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Brahma Sūtra methodology combining śruti and yukti
