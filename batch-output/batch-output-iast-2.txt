# अधिभूत
**Transliteration**: a-dhi-bhū-ta
**POS**: noun (neuter)

**Root Analysis**:
- adhi- (prefix) "above, over" + √bhū (bhū) "to be, become" + -ta (suffix) = "that which is above beings"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: relating to material elements, physical aspect
- Contextual: one of three aspects of reality in Vedanta
- Symbolic: gross physical manifestation of consciousness

**Learning Tip**:
- Remember as the physical/elemental aspect in the threefold classification

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: One of three aspects: adhidaiva, adhibhūta, adhyātma
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Gross body (sthūla śarīra) focus
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Material manifestation of cosmic principles
- **Mantric Significance**: Mantras for physical well-being and material harmony
- **Dharmic Significance**: Understanding matter as consciousness manifestation
- **Symbolic Representation**: Earth element, physical forms in mandalas
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Bhagavad Gītā

# अहम्
**Transliteration**: a-ham
**POS**: pronoun (first person singular)

**Root Analysis**:
- Not derived from a verbal root. Functions as a primary pronoun.

**Meanings**:
- Literal: I, self, ego
- Contextual: individual consciousness, ego-principle
- Symbolic: false identification vs. true Self

**Learning Tip**:
- Distinguish between aham (ego-I) and Aham (true Self)

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Individual consciousness principle, jīva identification
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Ego dissolution in higher consciousness
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Aham Brahmāsmi realization
- **Mantric Significance**: So'ham mantra, Aham brahmāsmi mahāvākya
- **Dharmic Significance**: Transcendence of ego for Self-realization
- **Symbolic Representation**: Individual soul (jīva) in cosmic diagrams
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Upaniṣads (mahāvākyas)
