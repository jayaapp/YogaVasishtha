# आं
**Transliteration**: āṃ
**POS**: sacred syllable/interjection

**Root Analysis**:
- Extended form of ॐ (AUM) with prolonged nasalization, representing cosmic sound with emphasis on transcendence

**Meanings**:
- Literal: Extended sacred syllable, prolonged AUM
- Contextual: Invocational sound used at beginning of mantras in Yoga Vasishtha
- Symbolic: Cosmic consciousness with emphasis on the transcendent aspect

**Learning Tip**:
- The prolonged nasalization (anusvāra) emphasizes the transcendent fourth state

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Cosmic sound extending into transcendent silence
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Extended chanting for deeper meditative states
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Brahman as cosmic sound extending beyond manifestation
- **Mantric Significance**: Preparatory sound for deep mantra practice
- **Dharmic Significance**: Bridge between formed sound and formless consciousness
- **Symbolic Representation**: ॐ with extended resonance or echo
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Tantric and meditation texts, Yoga Vasishtha

# आकार
**Transliteration**: ā-kā-ra
**POS**: noun (masculine)

**Root Analysis**:
- आ (ā) prefix + कार (kāra) from √कृ (kṛ) "to make" = "form, shape, appearance"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: Form, shape, appearance, configuration
- Contextual: Divine forms and cosmic shapes described in Yoga Vasishtha
- Symbolic: The principle of manifestation giving form to the formless

**Learning Tip**:
- आकार refers to visible form, while स्वरूप refers to essential nature

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: The principle of manifestation that gives form to consciousness
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Visualization practices using divine forms
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Divine manifestations in various forms and aspects
- **Mantric Significance**: Visualization of deity forms during mantra practice
- **Dharmic Significance**: Understanding form as expression of formless consciousness
- **Symbolic Representation**: Geometric forms in yantras and sacred art
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Yoga Vasishtha, texts on deity visualization
