# Vidūratha
**Transliteration**: Vi-dū-ra-tha
**POS**: proper noun (masculine)

**Root Analysis**:
- vi- (prefix) + dūra ("far, distant") + -tha (suffix) = "one who goes far" or "far-going"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: proper name, possibly meaning "far-going" or "distant chariot"
- Contextual: character name in Yoga Vasishtha
- Symbolic: N/A

**Learning Tip**:
- Remember as a character in the philosophical narratives

# अधिभूत
**Transliteration**: a-dhi-bhū-ta
**POS**: noun (neuter)

**Root Analysis**:
- adhi- (prefix) "above, over" + √bhū (bhū) "to be, become" + -ta (suffix) = "that which is above beings"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: relating to material elements, physical aspect
- Contextual: one of three aspects of reality in Vedanta
- Symbolic: gross physical manifestation of consciousness

**Learning Tip**:
- Remember as the physical/elemental aspect in the threefold classification

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: One of three aspects: adhidaiva, adhibhūta, adhyātma
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Gross body (sthūla śarīra) focus
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Material manifestation of cosmic principles
- **Mantric Significance**: Mantras for physical well-being and material harmony
- **Dharmic Significance**: Understanding matter as consciousness manifestation
- **Symbolic Representation**: Earth element, physical forms in mandalas
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Bhagavad Gītā

--- WORD DELIMITER ---

# अहम्
**Transliteration**: a-ham
**POS**: pronoun (first person singular)

**Root Analysis**:
- Not derived from a verbal root. Functions as a primary pronoun.

**Meanings**:
- Literal: I, self, ego
- Contextual: individual consciousness, ego-principle
- Symbolic: false identification vs. true Self

**Learning Tip**:
- Distinguish between aham (ego-I) and Aham (true Self)

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Individual consciousness principle, jīva identification
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Ego dissolution in higher consciousness
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Aham Brahmāsmi realization
- **Mantric Significance**: So'ham mantra, Aham brahmāsmi mahāvākya
- **Dharmic Significance**: Transcendence of ego for Self-realization
- **Symbolic Representation**: Individual soul (jīva) in cosmic diagrams
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Upaniṣads (mahāvākyas)

# अज्ञानहंकारयोरेको
**Transliteration**: aj-ñā-na-haṃ-kā-ra-yo-re-ko
**POS**: compound phrase

**Root Analysis**:

- Literal: the unity/oneness of ignorance and ego

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Core principle of bondage in Vedanta
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Understanding this unity helps in liberation practices
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Māyā as both veiling (ignorance) and projecting (ego) power
- **Mantric Significance**: Mantras addressing root of ignorance-ego complex
- **Dharmic Significance**: Fundamental obstacle to Self-realization
- **Symbolic Representation**: Single veil creating dual effects
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Advaita Vedanta commentaries

**Root Analysis**:
- duḥ "bad, difficult" + kha "space, opening" = "bad space, suffering"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: suffering, pain, distress, unhappiness
- Contextual: fundamental characteristic of conditioned existence
- Symbolic: inevitable result of ignorance and attachment

**Learning Tip**:
- One of the four noble truths, fundamental to understanding liberation

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Inherent characteristic of saṃsāric existence
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Motivation for spiritual practice and liberation
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Aspect overcome through divine grace
- **Mantric Significance**: Mantras for alleviating suffering
- **Dharmic Significance**: First noble truth, motivates spiritual seeking
- **Symbolic Representation**: Thorns, fire, bondage, darkness
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Buddhist texts, Sāṅkhya philosophy

# हृदयग्रन्थि
**Transliteration**: hṛ-da-ya-gran-thi
**POS**: compound noun (masculine)

**Root Analysis**:
- hṛdaya "heart" + granthi "knot" = "heart knot"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: heart knot, knot in the heart
- Contextual: psycho-spiritual blockage in the heart center
- Symbolic: fundamental knot of ego-identification

**Learning Tip**:
- The primary spiritual knot that must be dissolved for liberation

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Primary knot binding individual consciousness
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Major target in Kuṇḍalinī and heart-centered practices
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Dissolved by grace of guru or chosen deity
- **Mantric Significance**: Mantras specifically for dissolving heart knot
- **Dharmic Significance**: Liberation occurs when this knot is severed
- **Symbolic Representation**: Knot in the heart region, tight constriction
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Kuṇḍalinī yoga texts, Upaniṣads
