# अ
**Transliteration**: a
**POS**: vowel (phoneme), indefinite article, prefix

**Root Analysis**:
- Independent phoneme (varṇa), not formed from smaller components. Functions as the first vowel of the Sanskrit alphabet and as a negative prefix.

**Meanings**:
- Literal: The vowel 'a'; negation when used as prefix; indefinite article
- Contextual: Fundamental sound in Yoga Vasishtha representing primordial consciousness
- Symbolic: Represents Brahmā, creation, waking state (jāgrat), and the gross body

**Learning Tip**:
- Remember as the starting point of all Sanskrit sounds and the foundation of AUM (ॐ)

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Represents rajas guṇa and viśva (universal consciousness)
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Associated with mūlādhāra cakra and the gross realm
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Brahmā, the creator aspect of the divine trinity
- **Mantric Significance**: First component of the sacred praṇava (ॐ), represents creation
- **Dharmic Significance**: Symbol of manifestation and the beginning of cosmic expression
- **Symbolic Representation**: Often depicted as red in color in mantric contexts
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Māṇḍūkya Upaniṣad

# अउओम॰
**Transliteration**: a-u-o-m
**POS**: mantra compound

**Root Analysis**:
- Compound of vowels अ (a) + उ (u) + ओ (o) + म (m), representing the complete praṇava mantra

**Meanings**:
- Literal: The phonetic components of ॐ (AUM)
- Contextual: Sacred sound formula used in Yoga Vasishtha for meditation and invocation
- Symbolic: Complete representation of all states of consciousness and cosmic manifestation

**Learning Tip**:
- Practice pronunciation by slowly transitioning through each sound component

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Encompasses all three guṇas and four states of consciousness
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Complete cakra activation through sound vibration
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Trinity of Brahmā-Viṣṇu-Śiva plus transcendent Brahman
- **Mantric Significance**: The supreme bīja mantra containing all other mantras
- **Dharmic Significance**: Represents the totality of existence and consciousness
- **Symbolic Representation**: Often written as ॐ symbol in sacred texts
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Māṇḍūkya Upaniṣad, Yoga Vasishtha
