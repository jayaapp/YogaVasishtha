# कामिनां
**Transliteration**: kā-mi-nāṃ
**POS**: noun (masculine, genitive plural)

**Root Analysis**:
- √कम् (kam) "to desire" + इन् (in) suffix + genitive plural = "of the desirous ones"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: Of the desirous ones, of those who have desires
- Contextual: Those bound by desires discussed in Yoga Vasishtha as obstacles to liberation
- Symbolic: Souls trapped in the cycle of desire and attachment

**Learning Tip**:
- कामिन् means "desirous" or "lustful" - those driven by kāma (desire)

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Souls bound by desire in the cosmic web of attachment
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Condition to be transcended through spiritual practice
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Beings under influence of Kāma (desire deity)
- **Mantric Significance**: Mantras for overcoming desire and attachment
- **Dharmic Significance**: State of bondage requiring spiritual transformation
- **Symbolic Representation**: Figures bound by chains of desire
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Yoga Vasishtha, texts on overcoming desire
