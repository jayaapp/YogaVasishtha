# अधिभूम्या
**Transliteration**: a-dhi-bhū-myá
**POS**: adjective
**Root Analysis**:
- adhi- "over, above" + √bhū (bhū) "to be" + -ya suffix = "relating to what is above the earth"
**Meanings**:
- Literal: relating to the earthly realm, terrestrial
- Contextual: one of the threefold aspects of existence (adhibhūta)
- Symbolic: material plane of manifestation
**Learning Tip**:
- Remember as the material/earthly aspect in the threefold cosmic classification
**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: One of the three fundamental divisions of existence (adhibhūta, adhidaiva, adhyātma)
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Represents the gross physical realm in meditation practices
- **Dharmic Significance**: Foundation level of spiritual understanding before progressing to subtle realms
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Bhagavad Gītā (Chapter 8, verses on the threefold division)

# अहंकर्ता
**Transliteration**: a-haṃ-kar-ttá
**POS**: noun (masculine)
**Root Analysis**:
- ahaṃ "I" + √kṛ (kar) "to do, make" + -tṛ (agent suffix) = "one who makes the 'I'"
**Meanings**:
- Literal: the ego-maker, one who creates the sense of "I"
- Contextual: the principle that creates individual identity and separateness
- Symbolic: the mechanism of self-identification that creates bondage
**Learning Tip**:
- Think of it as the "I-maker" - the function that constantly reinforces ego-identity
**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Function within antaḥkaraṇa (inner instrument) that creates individuation
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: What must be transcended in advanced meditation states
- **Dharmic Significance**: Root cause of saṃsāra through identification with the non-Self
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Yoga Vāsiṣṭha (extensive discussions on ahaṃkāra)
