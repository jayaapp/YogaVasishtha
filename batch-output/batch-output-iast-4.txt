# गच्छत्यायुस्तदपि
**Transliteration**: gac-cha-tyā-yus-ta-da-pi
**POS**: compound phrase

**Root Analysis**:
- √gam (gam) "to go" + compound with āyus "life" + tad "that" + api "also" = "that life also goes"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: that life/age also goes/passes
- Contextual: philosophical statement about the transitory nature of life
- Symbolic: impermanence of individual existence

**Learning Tip**:
- Expresses the universal truth of life's temporary nature

# हृदयग्रन्थि
**Transliteration**: hṛ-da-ya-gran-thi
**POS**: compound noun (masculine)

**Root Analysis**:
- hṛdaya "heart" + granthi "knot" = "heart knot"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: heart knot, psychic knot in the heart
- Contextual: energetic blockage in the heart center
- Symbolic: spiritual obstacle to liberation

**Learning Tip**:
- One of the three main granthis (knots) that bind consciousness

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Major psychic obstruction preventing Self-realization
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Primary target for dissolution in advanced practice
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Viṣṇu granthi, related to attachment and emotions
- **Mantric Significance**: Mantras for dissolving heart-knot
- **Dharmic Significance**: Major obstacle requiring spiritual practice to dissolve
- **Symbolic Representation**: Knot, blockage, constriction in heart
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Yoga texts, liberation literature

# कर्मेन्द्रिय
**Transliteration**: kar-men-dri-ya
**POS**: compound noun (neuter)

**Root Analysis**:
- karma "action" + indriya "sense organ" = "action organ, motor organ"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: organ of action, motor organ
- Contextual: five organs of action in Sāṅkhya philosophy
- Symbolic: instruments of soul's interaction with world

**Learning Tip**:
- Five action organs: speech, hands, feet, excretion, reproduction

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Five motor organs in Sāṅkhya anatomical system
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Control and purification of action organs
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Deities governing different action organs
- **Mantric Significance**: Mantras for purifying action organs
- **Dharmic Significance**: Ethical use of action organs in dharmic living
- **Symbolic Representation**: Five-fold division of active powers
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Sāṅkhya Kārikā, philosophical anatomy texts
