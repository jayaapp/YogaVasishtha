# चित्तं
**Transliteration**: cit-taṃ
**POS**: noun [neuter], accusative singular

**Root Analysis**:
- √चित् (cit) – "to perceive, be aware, think" + त (ta) – "participial suffix" = "that which perceives; consciousness"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: mind, consciousness, heart, thought
- Contextual: The individual mind or consciousness principle in Vedantic psychology
- Symbolic: The mirror of awareness that reflects all mental modifications and experiences

**Learning Tip**:
- Remember चित्तं as the "cinema screen" of consciousness where all thoughts project

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Antaḥkaraṇa (inner instrument) component, subtle mind principle
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Primary focus of meditation and mental purification practices
- **Deity or Energy Form**: Reflects Cit-śakti, the consciousness aspect of divine energy
- **Mantric Significance**: Core concept in contemplative mantras focusing on awareness
- **Dharmic Significance**: Central to understanding the nature of individual consciousness versus universal consciousness
- **Symbolic Representation**: Often depicted as a lake or mirror in yogic imagery
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Yoga Sūtras of Patañjali, chapter on cittavṛttinirodhah

# चित्तनिबृत्ति
**Transliteration**: cit-ta-ni-bṛt-ti
**POS**: noun [feminine], compound term

**Root Analysis**:
- चित्त (citta) – "consciousness" + नि (ni) – "cessation prefix" + √वृत् (vṛt) – "to turn, exist" + ति (ti) – "action suffix" = "cessation of mental activity"

**Meanings**:
- Literal: restraint or cessation of mental modifications
- Contextual: The yogic state of mental stillness described in classical texts
- Symbolic: The calming of the ocean of consciousness to achieve perfect reflection

**Learning Tip**:
- Connect with निरोध (nirodha) - both indicate stopping mental fluctuations

**Metaphysics**:
- **Cosmic Role**: Essential stage in realizing the true Self beyond mental modifications
- **Energetic/Yogic Application**: Goal of dharana, dhyana, and samadhi practices
- **Deity or Energy Form**: State associated with Śiva's aspect as destroyer of illusion
- **Mantric Significance**: Achieved through concentrated japa and breath control
- **Dharmic Significance**: Prerequisite for discriminating between Puruṣa and Prakṛti
- **Symbolic Representation**: Still water reflecting the sky without distortion
- **Primary Textual Attestation**: Yoga Vasishtha, sections on mental control and liberation
